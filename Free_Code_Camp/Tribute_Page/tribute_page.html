Earl Nightingale

"We become what we think about."

Earl Nightingale's Journey

  • 1921: Born March 12
  • 1933: Father abandoned the family and his Mother moved them to a Tent in Tent City
  • 1938: Joined the Marine Corp
  • 1941: Was on the USS Arizona during Pearl Harbor and was 1 of 15 solders that survived the attack that were aboard.
  • 1949: Perspective on life changes after reading the book "Think and Grow Rich"
  • 1950: Voice of Sky King, the hero in a Radio Adventure series.
  • 1950-1956: WGN radio program host.
  • 1956: Released the 'Strangest Secret' which sells over 1 million copies, becoming the first spoken word to earn a Golden Record.
  • 1960: Releases an audio version of "Think and Grow Rich" and co-founds Nightingale-Conant Corporation.
  • 1976: he won the Golden Gavel Award from Toastmasters International and inducted into the National Speakers Association Speaker Hall of Fame.
  • 1987: Releases "Lead the Field" and "Earl Nightingale's Greatest Discoveries"
  • 1985: Nightingale was inducted into The National Association of Broadcasters National Radio Hall of Fame.
  • 1989: Dies after complications during heart surgery

Notable Facts

  • Terry Savage has termed the Strangest Secret “one of the great motivational books of all time“.
  • Nightingale wrote and recorded more than 7,000 radio programs, 250 audio programs as well as television programs and videos.
  • Nightingale’s radio program, Our Changing World, became the most syndicated radio program ever, and was broadcast across the USA, Canada, Mexico, Australia, New Zealand, Fiji, South Africa, the Bahamas, and 23 additional overseas countries, as well as the Armed Forces Network.
